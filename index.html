Session 3 • Gamma

Prompts you can steal today

presentationCreate a 10-slide seminar presentation on 'Edge Computing' for 2nd-year CSE students. One idea per slide, minimal text, a visual on each.
from notesTurn these notes into a 12-slide deck with an agenda, sections and a summary slide. [paste notes]
reportWrite a 2-page project report on my mini-project: problem, approach, results, conclusion. Formal tone. [paste details]
refineRestyle this deck with a clean, minimal theme and consistent heading sizes.
Name the audience, task, topic and style — vague prompts give generic decks.

Session 3 • Hands-on

Activity — build your seminar deck

The lab in one line: pick a topic → generate with Gamma → refine theme & visuals → export a PDF to submit.
  1. On gamma.app: generate a 10–12 slide deck on your seminar topic (audience + one idea per slide + a visual on each).
  2. Refine: pick a clean theme, trim text to phrases, add title & summary slides.
  3. In Figma: sketch a wireframe / mockup of your title screen.
  4. Export as PDF (8–12 slides).
📸 Keep screenshots as you go — title slide, one content slide, and your Figma sketch. You'll submit them.

Session 3 • Deliverable

What to submit — and the bar to clear

  • The Gamma share link to your deck
  • The deck exported as PDF (8–12 slides)
  • Screenshots: title slide + one content slide
  • One Figma wireframe / mockup screenshot
  • 3-line reflection: what AI did well, what you fixed by hand
One idea per slide. Strong contrast, consistent fonts, a visual beats a paragraph — and never ship placeholder text.
