Session 3 • Motivation

Why this matters

Professional communication and visual presentation are core academic and industry skills. Your idea is only as good as your ability to present it clearly.

The problem: making things look good — layout, spacing, colour, consistency — usually eats the time you meant to spend on the content.

AI design tools flip that: they handle the layout and polish so you spend your time on the message.

Session 3 • Hands-on · Task 1

Generate your presentation (Gamma)

  1. Go to gamma.app and sign in; choose Presentation.
  2. Pick a seminar topic from your branch (or one assigned).
  3. Prompt it: audience + 10–12 slides + one idea per slide + a visual on each.
  4. Let Gamma generate the first draft.
  5. Pick a clean theme and make heading sizes consistent.
📸 Keep a title slide + one content slide screenshot for your submission, and grab the share link.

Session 3 • Hands-on · Task 2

Refine, then add a design touch

  1. Trim every slide to one idea — cut paragraphs to phrases.
  2. Add or swap a visual (image / chart / icon) on each key slide.
  3. Add a clear title slide and a summary / thank-you slide.
  4. In Figma, sketch a simple wireframe or mockup of your title screen.
  5. Export the deck as PDF (8–12 slides).
Aim for a deck you could actually present — not a wall of text with a nice background.

Session 3 • Best practice

Design like it's yours, not the tool's

do

Make it clear

One idea per slide; strong contrast; consistent fonts & spacing; align things to a grid; a visual beats a paragraph.

don't

Ship the first draft

Don't leave placeholder text; don't mix five fonts and colours; don't paste dense paragraphs; don't use an image that adds nothing.

AI gives you a fast, decent draft — taste and editing are what make it good.
